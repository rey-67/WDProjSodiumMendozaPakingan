SurviveBlocks: Bosses

Ender Dragon

The Ender Dragon is the main final boss and the last step to completing the game's storyline. It flies around the main End island and uses several attacks such as dragon breath clouds, fireballs, knockback dives, and wing hits that push the player into the air or off the island. Dragon breath lingers on the ground and deals heavy damage, so players must avoid standing in it.

Before dealing real damage to the dragon, players must destroy the end crystals on top of obsidian pillars. Some crystals are protected by iron bars, which require players to climb up or shoot carefully through gaps. Once the crystals are gone, the dragon can no longer heal, making it possible to start the real fight.

When the dragon perches on the exit portal, it becomes resistant to arrows, so players must use a sword or axe to deal damage. Critical hits and sweeping attacks help, but players must stand to the side to avoid taking too much knockback. Beds can also explode in the End, which advanced players use for extremely high damage if timed correctly.

After defeating the dragon, players unlock access to the End gateway, which leads to the outer islands. These islands contain end cities, shulkers that drop shells for shulker boxes, enchanted loot, and elytras for flight. Players can also respawn the Ender Dragon by placing four end crystals on the portal frame if they want more experience or if they missed the dragon egg.

Warden

The Warden is the strongest mob in the game and is found only in the Deep Dark biome. It does not naturally exist in the world unless the player activates sculk shriekers repeatedly. When it appears, the surrounding area becomes dark, and the Warden becomes more aggressive if it senses movement or sound.

The Warden uses two main types of attacks. The first is its melee attack, which can kill most players instantly even with enchanted diamond or netherite armor. The second is the sonic boom, a ranged attack that goes through walls, shields, armor, and blocks. This makes hiding behind walls useless because the sonic boom hits regardless of cover.

The safest strategy is to avoid the Warden completely. Sneaking, walking on wool blocks, and throwing snowballs to distract it help players move without being detected. Ancient Cities contain extremely valuable loot such as enchanted diamond armor, enchanted books, echo shards for the recovery compass, swift sneak enchantments, redstone contraptions, and unique structures that cannot be found anywhere else.

While it is technically possible to kill the Warden, it has so much health that the process is slow, risky, and not worth it for most players. It drops no special loot, which reinforces that the Warden is more of a survival challenge than a boss that players are meant to defeat.

Elder Guardian

Elder Guardians are giant versions of regular guardians and appear in ocean monuments. There are normally three of them in each monument, each located in a different sealed room. They attack with a laser beam that charges up over a short period and deals heavy damage if the player does not break the beam in time.

Another major threat is Mining Fatigue III, which Elder Guardians repeatedly apply to the player. This effect makes breaking blocks extremely slow, making it almost impossible to dig through the monument without milk or specialized tools. Because of this, players often bring milk buckets to counter the effect or use doors, sponges, and potions to navigate more easily.

To prepare for this fight, players often bring water breathing potions, night vision potions to see underwater clearly, Depth Strider boots, Respiration helmets, or a trident if they have one. These items help players swim faster, breathe longer, and attack more effectively.

Clearing a monument gives access to sponges, prismarine blocks, sea lanterns, and a hidden room filled with gold blocks. These resources make monuments one of the best locations for underwater building, farming guardians, or collecting rare blocks for decoration and lighting.

Wither

The Wither is a boss that players summon themselves. To spawn it, players place soul sand in a T shape and put three wither skeleton skulls on top. After spawning, the Wither charges up, becomes invincible for a few seconds, and then explodes with a huge blast that damages anything nearby.

The Wither fires two kinds of skulls: regular skulls that explode, and blue skulls that break almost any block they hit. The blue skulls travel slower but cause more destruction, making the terrain extremely dangerous during the fight. Players also suffer from the Wither effect, which drains health and turns the health bar black, making it harder to track health.

When the Wither reaches half health, it activates Wither Armor, which makes it immune to arrows. At this point, players must use swords or axes to finish the fight. Strength potions, golden apples, enchanted armor, and regeneration potions are very effective in surviving this stage.

Many players summon the Wither underground in a tunnel or in the End on top of bedrock to limit how much it can fly around. After defeating it, the Wither drops a nether star, which is required to craft a beacon. Beacons give players buffs like Haste II for mining, Strength for combat, or Speed for faster travel, making them one of the most valuable tools for building large bases and farms.
