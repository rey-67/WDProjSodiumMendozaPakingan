SurviveBlocks: Dimensions

The Overworld

Temperate Biomes

Plains – Plains are flat and easy to build on, which is why many players choose them for their main base. Villages often spawn here, along with animals like cows, sheep, and horses. This makes plains one of the best biomes to start in.

Forest – Forests are one of the most useful biomes early on. They provide plenty of wood, mostly oak and birch, and animals spawn frequently. This makes it easier to get food and basic materials on the first few days.

Taiga – Taiga biomes have spruce trees and wolves. Some versions are snowy, but villages can still appear. Spruce wood is commonly used for darker builds and large structures.

Warm Biomes

Desert – Deserts are made of sand and have very little vegetation. Desert villages and temples can generate here. Temples sometimes contain valuable loot, but they can be dangerous if you are not careful.

Savanna – Savannas have acacia trees and wide open spaces. Villages spawn often, and the terrain usually has tall cliffs and plateaus, making it easier to spot structures from far away.

Jungle – Jungles are dense and harder to move through. They contain vines, bamboo, melons, and cocoa beans. Jungle temples can generate here, and parrots can also be found.

Cold and Mountain Biomes

Snowy Plains – Snowy plains are covered in snow and ice. There are fewer animals and plants, which can make surviving harder at the start. Snow and ice can still be useful for building or redstone.

Snowy Taiga – This is a colder version of the taiga biome. Wolves still spawn here, and villages can appear, but gathering food can take more time.

Mountain Biomes – Mountain biomes are tall and steep. Coal and iron are often visible on the surface, which makes early mining easier, but moving around can be risky.

Ocean Biomes

Ocean – Oceans contain fish, drowned mobs, and shipwrecks. Shipwrecks usually have chests with food, iron, and maps.

Warm Ocean – Warm oceans have coral reefs and tropical fish. These biomes are mostly decorative but are useful for collecting coral and sea-related items.

Frozen Ocean – Frozen oceans have icebergs and frozen water. Travel is slower and more dangerous, especially with drowned mobs nearby.

What You Can Find and Do in the Overworld

The Overworld is where most of the game takes place. Players gather basic resources like wood, stone, iron, and diamonds. This is where you build your main base, explore caves, trade with villagers, and prepare equipment before going to other dimensions.

The Nether

Nether Biomes

Nether Wastes – This is the most common Nether biome. It has large lava lakes, nether quartz, and zombified piglins. It is usually the first biome players see when entering the Nether.

Crimson Forest – Crimson forests are filled with piglins and hoglins. Hoglins can be used as a food source, and piglins can be bartered with gold for useful items.

Warped Forest – Warped forests are safer compared to other Nether biomes. Endermen spawn often here, making it a good place to collect ender pearls.

Soul Sand Valley – This biome slows down movement and spawns skeletons and ghasts frequently. It is dangerous and difficult to travel through.

Basalt Deltas – Basalt deltas have uneven terrain and magma cubes. Moving around is harder, and falling into lava is common.

What You Can Find and Do in the Nether

The Nether contains materials that cannot be found in the Overworld, such as glowstone, nether wart, and ancient debris. Players search for Nether Fortresses to get blaze rods, which are needed for potion brewing and reaching the End. Ancient debris is used to create netherite gear.

The End

End Biomes

The End (Main Island) – This is where the Ender Dragon fight happens. The island is surrounded by obsidian pillars with End crystals on top.

End Highlands – End Highlands are found on the outer islands. This is where End Cities generate.

End Midlands – These areas connect the larger islands. There is not much to find here.

End Barrens – Mostly empty islands with very little to explore.

Small End Islands – Small floating islands with chorus plants. Chorus fruit can teleport the player when eaten.

What You Can Find and Do in the End

The End is where players fight the Ender Dragon. After defeating it, players explore End Cities to find Elytra and shulker boxes. These items make traveling and storage much easier later in the game.
